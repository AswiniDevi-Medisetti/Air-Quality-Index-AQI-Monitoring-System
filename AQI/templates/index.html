About Air Quality Index

What is AQI?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is an index for reporting daily air quality. It tells you how clean or polluted your air is, and what associated health effects might be a concern for you.

How is AQI Calculated?

AQI is calculated based on the concentrations of major air pollutants:

  • PM2.5 - Fine particulate matter (diameter ≤ 2.5μm)
  • PM10 - Coarse particulate matter (diameter ≤ 10μm)
  • NO₂ - Nitrogen Dioxide
  • SO₂ - Sulfur Dioxide
  • CO - Carbon Monoxide
  • O₃ - Ozone

Health Impacts

AQI Category Health Impacts
Good (0-50) Minimal impact
Satisfactory (51-100) Minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people
Moderate (101-200) Breathing discomfort to people with lung disease
Poor (201-300) Breathing discomfort to most people on prolonged exposure
Very Poor (301-400) Respiratory illness on prolonged exposure
Severe (401-500) Affects healthy people and seriously impacts those with existing diseases
